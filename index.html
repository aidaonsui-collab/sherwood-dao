IV.The Vault · Loans

Your bag is a credit line.

Because backing is real, you can borrow against it. The Vault lends USDG against staked $WOOD at the backing floor — fixed rate, no expiry, and no price liquidations: the loan is written against what the treasury holds, not against what the market fears.

Sell nothing. Owe the Sheriff nothing.

V.The Greenwood Band · Range-Bound Stability

Price walks. Backing marches.

A premium left to wander is what breaks a reserve currency. Sherwood tethers price to backing in both directions from day one: below the band the treasury bids with real reserves; far above it, it sells into strength. The floor was tested once — 10 June — and held.

VI.The Ballads · Docs

The tokenomics, sung plainly.

Every mechanism in the protocol has a ballad, and every ballad ends in a moral you can verify.

The Ballad of the Bond

He brought the forest T-bills bright,
and laid them at the oak;
five days he waits, his WOOD then vests —
a discount for good folk.

Moral — A bond trades a discount for time. The treasury keeps the principal forever.

The Ballad of the Watch

The horn is blown, the spoils are split,
three watches to the day;
who holds his ground keeps all his share —
the rest is APY.

Moral — Rebases are emissions, not profit. What staking preserves is your fraction of the forest.

The Ballad of the Floor

The Sheriff sold the greenwood short
to break us at the bough;
the treasury bought his panic whole —
the floor is holding now.

Moral — Below the band the treasury bids with real assets; far above, it sells. Price walks; backing marches.
